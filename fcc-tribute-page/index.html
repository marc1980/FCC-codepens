Alan Turing

A man ahead of its time

Bio

  • 1912 - Alan Mathison Turing is born in London
  • 1922 - Hazelhurst Preparatory School
  • 1926 - Sherborne School
  • 1931 - King's College, Cambridge, first-class honours in mathematics
  • 1935 - Elected a fellow of King's
  • 1936 - Published his paper "On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem"
  • 1936 - Princeton University
  • 1938 - PhD from Princeton; dissertation: "Systems of Logic Based on Ordinals"
  • 1938 - Cryptanalyst GC&CS
  • 1939 - Cryptanalyst Bletchley Park, working on deciphering encrypred messages from the German army, with the "bombe" machine
  • 1942 - Cryptanalyst US Navy
  • 1943 - Return to Bletchley Park
  • 1945 - National Physical Laboratory (NPL), working on the design of the ACE (Automatic Computing Engine)
  • 1946 - Paper on the first detailed design of a stored-program computer
  • 1948 - Appointed Reader in the Mathematics Department at the Victoria University of Manchester
  • 1949 - Deputy Director of the Computing Machine Laboratory there, working on software for one of the earliest stored-program computers—the Manchester Mark 1.
  • 1951 - CHange to mathematical biology
  • 1952 - Publication of his masterpiece "The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis"
  • 1952 - Convicted for gross indecency for his relationship with Arnold Murray
  • 1954 - Died in Chesire on Cyanide poisoning
